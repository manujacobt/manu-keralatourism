Alappuzha

Referred to as the ‘Venice of the East’ by Lord Curzon, Alleppey is known for its backwaters, beaches and canals. But, the city is more than rustic Kerala backwaters and houseboats. The temples and ancient sites are an absolute delight for history buffs and anyone who loves art. If you are longing for a relaxed vacation away from the crowd, amidst nature, get ready to fall in love with Alleppey or Alappuzha

Alappuzha Beach

Also known as Alleppey Beach, it is one of the most popular attractions in the city. The scenic beach has a 150-year old pier merging into the sea. Alappuzha beach hosts many festivals including the famous Sand Art Festival. While the beach is loved by visitors who want to spend some quiet time, the place is also a hit among adventure lovers. You can try surfing, parasailing, and other activities on the beach.

Punnapra Beach

Hemmed by a dense treeline, this wide sandy beach is known for its many fishing boats and remote vibe. It is an isolated beach away from hustle and bustle of city life. The blue water and golden sandy beach is the main attraction of the visitors. Since the beach is not visited by a large number of visitors, so the beach is preferred by romantic lovers. The serenity of the beach adds to the beauty of the nature lovers and photographers in the golden sandy Punnapra Beach. Plan a trip to Alleppey for visiting the picturesque sandy beach. You must visit the beach with family and friends while in Alleppey of Kerala.

Vembanad Lake

The longest lake in India, Vembanad Lake flows across several districts in Kerala. It is called by several names including Punamada Lake, Kochi Lake, and Vembanadu Lake. Among the major highlights of this lake is Kettuvallams (houseboats), numerous resorts along the banks and the famous Nehru Trophy Boat Race.

Pathiramanal

Pathiramanal means ‘Sands of the Night’. A small lake island on the backwaters of Lake Vembanad, it is an ideal spot to observe birds. There are more than 91 species of local and 50 species of migratory birds. Explore the island and you will be surprised to find yourself surrounded by some rare species of birds and chirping sound coming from everywhere.

Marari Beach

Turquoise water, golden sand, and palm trees at the backdrop make Marari an ideal retreat for a beach vacation. Marari beach was named among the top five ‘Hammock Beaches in the World’ by National Geographic Survey. You can try deep-sea fishing, surfing, parasailing, and water-skiing at the beach.
